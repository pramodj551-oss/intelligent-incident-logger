SECTION 1: GENERAL PRINCIPLES
====================================================================

All security personnel must adhere to these protocols immediately upon
observing an incident. Chain of command: Guard → Shift Supervisor →
Control Room → Senior Security Manager → Client Liaison Officer.

Emergency Contact Numbers:
- Control Room (24x7):       022-XXXX-0001
- Bomb Detection Squad:      022-XXXX-0002
- Thane Fire Brigade:        101
- Thane Police:              100
- Medical Emergency:         102 / 108
- AP Securitas HQ:           1800-XXX-XXXX

Golden Rule: DO NOT TOUCH, DO NOT MOVE any suspicious object.
            Secure the area FIRST. Report SECOND. Assist THIRD.

Protocol 101: UNATTENDED OR SUSPICIOUS OBJECT
====================================================================

APPLICABLE WHEN: Any bag, package, box, vehicle or object is left
unattended without an identifiable owner for more than 5 minutes in
a sensitive area, OR if an object appears tampered, has unusual wires,
makes sounds, emits smell, or is at an unexpected location.

THREAT LEVEL: HIGH

IMMEDIATE ACTIONS (Execute in this exact order):
1. Do NOT touch, kick, move, or cover the suspicious object.
2. Clear the area — maintain a minimum 50-metre safety perimeter.
   Do not allow anyone to approach. Use cones/barriers if available.
3. Do NOT use mobile phones or radios within 15 metres of the object
   (radio frequencies can trigger certain detonators).
4. Notify Control Room immediately: state your name, exact location,
   description of object, time first seen, and current perimeter status.
5. Control Room will contact Bomb Detection Squad (BDS).
   Expected response time: 15–30 minutes from notification.
6. Keep the area clear — do not re-enter even if requested.
7. Provide a written Incident Report within 30 minutes of clearance.
8. Preserve CCTV footage of the area from 1 hour before discovery.

KEY PHRASE FOR RADIO REPORT:
"Code Silver — [Location] — Unattended object — Perimeter secured —
Requesting BDS dispatch — Time: [HH:MM]"

DO NOT:
- Do not attempt to identify or examine the object yourself.
- Do not cut any wires or strings attached to the object.
- Do not allow media or unauthorized persons near the perimeter.
- Do not clear Code Silver status without BDS confirmation.

Protocol 102: FIRE, SMOKE, OR EXPLOSION
====================================================================

APPLICABLE WHEN: Smoke is visually detected, fire alarm sounds,
smell of burning, visible flames, or an explosion is heard/felt.

THREAT LEVEL: HIGH

IMMEDIATE ACTIONS:
1. Activate the nearest fire alarm pull station (red break-glass unit).
2. Call Control Room: "Code Red — [Exact Location/Floor/Block] — Fire
   confirmed/suspected — Evacuation initiated."
3. Initiate building evacuation via public address system if available.
   Shout "FIRE — Please evacuate immediately — Use stairways — Do not
   use lifts" repeatedly along corridors.
4. Guide occupants to nearest assembly point:
   Block A/B → Assembly Point Alpha (North Parking)
   Block C/D → Assembly Point Beta (South Garden)
5. Do NOT use elevators during evacuation.
6. Perform a headcount at assembly point. Notify supervisor of any
   missing persons immediately.
7. Do not re-enter the building until Fire Brigade gives all-clear.
8. Fire Brigade contact: 101. Expected response: 8–12 minutes.

FIRE EXTINGUISHER GUIDE (for small contained fires only):
- Red (Water): Paper, wood, textile
- Black (CO2): Electrical equipment, server rooms
- Cream (Foam): Flammable liquids
- Blue (Powder): Multi-purpose

DO NOT attempt to fight a fire that is larger than a waste bin.
Evacuate first; extinguish only if fire is small and exit is clear.

Protocol 103: MEDICAL EMERGENCY
====================================================================

APPLICABLE WHEN: Person is unconscious, unresponsive, having a
seizure, cardiac event (chest pain, arm pain), difficulty breathing,
severe bleeding, or any life-threatening condition.

THREAT LEVEL: HIGH

IMMEDIATE ACTIONS:
1. Do NOT move the person unless they are in immediate danger.
2. Call 108 (Emergency Medical Services) immediately.
   State: location, number of casualties, nature of injury.
3. Notify Control Room: "Code Blue — [Location] — Medical emergency —
   EMS contacted — Requesting First Aid kit and AED."
4. If trained: Begin CPR if person is unresponsive and not breathing.
   AED locations: Main Lobby Level 1, Cafeteria Level 2, Block C Lobby.
5. Clear the area — give the person space and airflow.
6. Assign one guard to direct EMS to the exact location at building
   entrance. Do not leave the casualty unattended.
7. Do not give food, water, or medication to the casualty.
8. Preserve scene for medical investigation.

Protocol 104: UNAUTHORIZED ACCESS / INTRUDER
====================================================================

APPLICABLE WHEN: Person is found in a restricted area without valid
access credentials, or has forcibly entered (broken door/window), or
has evaded access control.

THREAT LEVEL: HIGH (armed) / MEDIUM (unarmed)

IMMEDIATE ACTIONS:
1. Do NOT physically confront an armed or violent intruder.
   Maintain safe distance. Observe and report.
2. Notify Control Room: "Code Orange — [Location] — Unauthorized
   individual — Description: [height/build/clothing/direction of travel]"
3. Control Room will alert Thane Police (100) if required.
4. If intruder is unarmed and cooperative: politely challenge,
   request ID, and escort to reception for verification.
5. If intruder is uncooperative: do not block exit; maintain visual
   contact; relay real-time movement to Control Room.
6. Secure adjacent access points — lock down nearest server rooms
   and asset-holding areas immediately.
7. Review access logs and CCTV. Preserve footage.
8. File an Incident Report with full physical description and timeline.

DESCRIPTION TEMPLATE (radio):
"Male/Female — Approximate age — Height — Build — Clothing colour
and type — Hair — Any distinguishing features — Last seen at [location]
heading [direction] — Time: [HH:MM]"

Protocol 105: ROBBERY OR VIOLENT CRIME
====================================================================

APPLICABLE WHEN: Theft with threat/force, assault, or any crime
involving violence or threat of violence on premises.

THREAT LEVEL: HIGH

IMMEDIATE ACTIONS:
1. GUARD SAFETY IS THE TOP PRIORITY — Do not resist if weapon is shown.
   Comply with robber's demands. Note every detail for later reporting.
2. Press the silent panic alarm button at your post (if available).
3. Once safe: immediately call Control Room and Police (100).
   State: "Code Red — Armed incident — [Location] — [Direction
   criminal fled] — Physical description — Weapon description."
4. Do not pursue the criminal.
5. Secure and preserve crime scene — do not allow anyone to touch
   or move anything.
6. Detain any witnesses present; take contact details.
7. Inform HR and Senior Security Manager within 15 minutes.
8. Provide a detailed written statement within 1 hour.

Protocol 106: BOMB THREAT (Telephone or Written)
====================================================================

APPLICABLE WHEN: Any call, email, message, or note threatening an
explosive device on the premises.

THREAT LEVEL: HIGH

IF RECEIVING A BOMB THREAT CALL:
1. Stay calm. Keep the caller talking as long as possible.
2. Use the Bomb Threat Checklist (posted at all reception desks).
   Note: exact words used, voice description, background sounds,
   time of call, number called from, any accent/speech pattern.
3. Do NOT hang up — signal a colleague to call police on another line.
4. Immediately notify Control Room and Senior Security Manager.
5. Control Room will decide whether to evacuate based on threat
   credibility assessment.
6. If evacuation ordered: Follow Protocol 102 evacuation procedure.
7. Do NOT re-enter building until Police/BDS gives all-clear.

Protocol 107: SUSPICIOUS VEHICLE
====================================================================

APPLICABLE WHEN: A vehicle is parked in a restricted/no-parking
zone for more than 30 minutes without authorization, appears
abandoned, has suspicious contents visible, or its occupants
are behaving suspiciously.

THREAT LEVEL: MEDIUM (abandoned vehicle = HIGH if suspicious contents)

IMMEDIATE ACTIONS:
1. Note full vehicle details: make, model, colour, registration number,
   number of occupants, and time first observed.
2. Attempt to locate owner via reception/parking management.
3. If owner not found within 30 minutes: notify Control Room.
4. If vehicle appears abandoned with unusual contents or wires visible:
   Treat as Protocol 101 (Suspicious Object). Clear 50m perimeter.
5. Do NOT attempt to open/enter the vehicle.
6. Preserve CCTV footage of vehicle entry into campus.

====================================================================

Protocol 108: SUSPECTED THEFT / MISSING PROPERTY
====================================================================

APPLICABLE WHEN: Employee reports property stolen from their desk,
common area, or vehicle; or valuable assets are found missing during
audit.

THREAT LEVEL: LOW (missing property) / MEDIUM (confirmed theft)

IMMEDIATE ACTIONS:
1. Take a detailed statement from the reporting person:
   - What was stolen / lost
   - Last known location and time
   - Estimated value
   - Any suspects or witnesses
2. Review CCTV footage for the relevant area and time window.
3. Do not disturb the area until CCTV review is complete.
4. Notify Security Supervisor and Client HR within 1 hour.
5. If high-value asset (laptop, mobile, server equipment): log with
   police (FIR may be required by client policy).
6. Fill Incident Report Form IT-SEC-04 and submit to Control Room.

====================================================================

Protocol 109: MISSING PERSON ON PREMISES
====================================================================

APPLICABLE WHEN: An employee, visitor, or contractor is reported
missing on campus and cannot be located.

THREAT LEVEL: MEDIUM

IMMEDIATE ACTIONS:
1. Confirm last-seen location and time from reporting person.
2. Check access control logs for all exits — verify if person has
   left campus.
3. Conduct a systematic search of common areas, washrooms, and
   stairwells with at least two guards.
4. Notify Control Room and Senior Security Manager immediately.
5. If person is not located within 20 minutes: call Police (100).
6. Do NOT share personal information of missing person with media.

====================================================================

Protocol 110: NATURAL DISASTER / EMERGENCY EVACUATION
====================================================================

APPLICABLE WHEN: Earthquake, flood, severe storm causing structural
damage, or any event requiring full campus evacuation.

THREAT LEVEL: HIGH

IMMEDIATE ACTIONS:
1. EARTHQUAKE: Instruct everyone to Drop, Cover, and Hold On.
   Do NOT evacuate during shaking — wait for shaking to stop.
   After shaking stops: orderly evacuation following Protocol 102 routes.
2. FLOOD/STORM: Move occupants to upper floors. Block ground-level
   access points. Notify BMC Disaster Management (1916).
3. All emergencies: Take visitor register and employee roll call list.
4. Account for all persons at assembly point.
5. Notify AP Securitas Emergency Coordinator.
6. Remain at assembly point until authority gives all-clear.
